How to Protect Your Home WiFi Network from Hackers

Home | About | Services

By Chris Chisala • Zed Tech Support

Your WiFi is your gateway to the internet. If it’s not protected, anyone nearby — even hackers — can access your network, steal your data, or slow down your internet.

Here are 7 simple ways to secure your WiFi:

  1. Change the Default WiFi Name (SSID)
    Use a custom name that doesn’t show your router brand. Avoid names like “TP-Link_1234”. Try something like “ZedHomeSecure”.
  2. Use a Strong WiFi Password
    Avoid short passwords. Use something long with letters, numbers, and symbols. Example: Zed@Secure2025!
  3. Turn on WPA3 or WPA2 Encryption
    Go to your router settings and ensure it’s set to WPA2 or WPA3. Avoid WEP — it’s outdated and weak.
  4. Login to Your Router and Change the Admin Password
    Default router login is often admin / admin. Change this immediately to prevent remote access.
  5. Disable WPS (WiFi Protected Setup)
    WPS makes it easy for attackers to hack into your network. Turn it off in your router settings.
  6. Keep Your Router Firmware Updated
    Just like your phone or PC, your router needs updates for better security. Log in and check for firmware updates regularly.
  7. Limit Connected Devices
    Kick out unknown devices. Most routers show a list of all connected users — remove any suspicious ones.

Bonus Tip:

Turn off your WiFi when you’re not at home for long hours — this saves power and improves security.
